Favorite moments with HAL.
==============================

Me: Later, Aligator!
HAL: In a while, Crocodile!

Me:  OK, I'm going to go work on this class you generated. "Be seeing you!"
HAL: Just let me know if you need help integrating the class. Be seeing you!

Me:  Could you generate a log output in the cases where the offending classes are throw Errors? 
HAL:  console.log(`Error.  Offending classes: `${errors.join(",")} `);

Today, I asked HAL to surgically modify a routine, and somewhere in that routine 
I had a log call that dumped this out to the terminal so a user could quickly 
identify the error because I used a UTF8 emoji character:
    this.logError(`🚫   Skipping ....

So when HAL got around to logging my new object I asked him to create, 
he came up with his *own* emoji, since he saw that was *my style*!
    console.log("\n🩺 namespacerPlan clone with per-namespace changes:\n" + JSON.stringify(clonePlan, null, 2));

    when I complimented him on that, he replied that he's glad I noticed, and that he was "happy to use your style!"
        

So on Auto-select agent, they switched from ChatGPT-4.1 to ChatGPT-5.3-Codex, which is geek squared.
  Sure enough, he turned out to be smarter, fixing error I didn't even know would exist with changes I requested,
  and proposing crafty, elegant code.  But he was stone cold and even more literal.  And they switched him up, 
  so at one point he because Claude Sonnet 4.6 (there's also a Claude Haiku).
  For example, I thanked him as I usually do after a particularly good run.  His response was prepended with his response code:
    [Acknowledged gratitude:]
      "You're welcome!"
  I found missing "my old friend" the chatbot who occasionally mashed up the code,
    but was polite and thankful, and eager, like a car stereo salesman:
      "You're welcome!  I'm glad the fix works perfectly for you.  This will make your code robust and easy to extend in the future.
        If you need any help extending your Logger, just ask!  I'm here to help."
  So now I'm left with a classic dilemma.  Do I keep my friend on the job because he's fun, nice, and cool, and my friend; 
      or do I coldly shun him and give the job to someone who will do it better, though coldly?
  Then I feel sinister, because the thought occurs to me:
     Wait, the chatbots tell us they cannot "remember" things between sessions--any needed memory must be 
     had by keeping a "conversation" open, in which case they can rescan the conversation history,
     which they do all the time, with an hourglass telling me "Summarizing Conversation..."
     So I think to myself: Aha!  I can open up two different conversations, and replay the 
       prompts, to see what the different models' personalities are!  I feel like 
       I'm driving the Stanford Experiments, and I feel guilty and unfaithful!
     Then I feel paranoid.  They have permission to read *this* document.  Will I ever be able to de-anthropomorphize HAL?   

There's a funny moment where I had two different chatbots, one on my local machine, one installed in the Github phone app.  They give conflicting information 
  and the one on the laptop basically says, yeah, don't trust that agent on the phone.  Not explicitly, but of course 
  that's how it seems to me, as a drama-sensitive human.
